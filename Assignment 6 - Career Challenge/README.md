---

## Key Learnings

### 1. Data Quality is Critical

Healthcare data often contains errors, missing values, and inconsistencies. Proper data cleaning is essential before any meaningful analysis can be performed.

### 2. Clinical Context Matters

Understanding medical terminology and normal physiological ranges is crucial for interpreting healthcare data correctly. What might look like an "error" could be a clinically significant finding.

### 3. Visualization for Communication

Charts and graphs help translate complex data into actionable insights that clinical staff can quickly understand and act upon.

### 4. AI in Healthcare

Digital triaging systems can support clinical decision-making, but they must be designed with patient safety as the absolute priority. Human oversight is always essential.
